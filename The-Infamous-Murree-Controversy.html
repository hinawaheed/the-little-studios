The Infamous Murree Controversy

Murree as most people know is no. 1 tourist attraction among the people of Pakistan with its amazing weather, snow-capped mountains, pine trees, and amazing food. However, this beautiful place got caught in one of the biggest scandals aka controversy.


It all started with a couple of videos going viral on social media platforms. The content of the video was tourists being abused in Murree verbally. That started a chain of messages from the people that faced similar experiences.


The threads of messages contained one common complaint, overpriced food, and hotels. The tourists also shared the receipts of food and hotel bills to confirm their claim. According to their claim, the basic lunch of chicken karahi, pakoras, chapati, and green tea costed tourists around Rs. 45,000 as if the chapati was made of gold or the chicken was the last chicken that remained on earth. All of the injustices people faced gave birth to the hashtag #Boycottmurree. Within a few hours, the hashtag started trending no. 1 on Twitter Pakistan.


The hashtag alone caused a massive frenzy among the netizens and they vowed to not even enter Murree at-least for a few months. The roads of Murree which were once jam-packed with tourists started looking like a barren land. Only in a couple of hours, #Boycottmurree backlashed pretty badly, Murree went from the busiest place to an empty, deserted place.


Within two days, Murree became empty as the tourists that were there in the first place left. Within a week, the prices of food came significantly down as no one was visiting Murree causing significant loss for the street vendors whose only source of income was tourists. Not only that but the places where parking a car cost you a hefty sum of money, became available for free.


Murree is the place only place in Pakistan that has seen all sorts of occasions, from honeymoon to dates, and from school trips to family trips. And tourism was the only thing that made Murree significant. However, tourism comes with responsibility. The responsibility to make the tourist spot as clean as possible and providing the tourist with basic facilities of toilet and parking. Nevertheless, the Murree administration has been turning blind eye to its needs. There is no tourist body in Murree that make sure Murree is up the mark. The streets of Murree which were once lined up with beautiful and colorful umbrellas are now lined up with heaps of garbage. The basic necessity like toilets is lacking. The toilets are either malfunctioning or if they are working properly, they are too dirty to be used. There are no parking lots in Murree so tourists have no choice but to park the cars where they feel like which end up in blockage of roads. The government (judging from the recent chaos) has other matters to deal with and consider tourism a trivial matter.


Trying to meet unrealistic expectations of the world, deadlines on top of deadlines, binging on chai/coffee, and finding solace in the food is a common thing nowadays. And when chai/coffee or the deliciousness of food stops working as an escape from reality, that is where people pack their bags and become a temporary tourist. But what if your escape from reality cost you more than the salary you earned. And you come home traumatized, penny-less and injured.


With the current condition of the government, and no one questioning the authorities about why Murree is being the way it is, we are the only hope Murree has. We have to make Murree the way it was and together we can.
