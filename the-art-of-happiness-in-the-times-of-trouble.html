The Art of Happiness

When was the last time you smiled? Not for the camera, but actual genuine smile that came from you being happy and tuned.

Can’t think of any occasion?

Gottcha!


But, you are not alone. In our day to day lives, we actively do things that rob us of our happiness and then wonder why on earth we cannot feel happy.

Happiness is a subjective feeling and it’s like a smiley balloon. The bigger the balloon, the more people will be able to notice it. However, there is one thing that can shrink the smiley balloon. Can you guess it?


Yes, it’s Holes, Thumbs Up to you if you guessed it right!


As much as you think that other people are the reason for poking holes in your happiness balloon, it’s not. It’s You who actively poke holes in your happiness balloon.


Below are the needles that you use to poke holes in your happiness balloon.


Devaluing Happiness: Yes, you constantly devalue happiness without noticing it. How? Imagine yourself at a pricey and fancy buffet. You can eat anything there but there is a section in which you can choose one of the two options. Beans or Wagyu Beef. You love beans and dislike wagyu beef. Which would you choose, keeping in view that the buffet is pricey? Wagyu beef or beans?

If you choose wagyu beef, you poked a hole in your happiness balloon because you chose a value for money instead of happiness. Shocking, right? Well, you have been doing it all your life.

Pro tip: Choose happiness over value for money, goals, money, etc.


Superiority: Are you constantly edgy, on the go, don’t find time to even look at yourself in the mirror? If yes, you are chasing superiority by constantly comparing yourself to other people in terms of beauty, lifestyle, money, happiness, etc. It is not only poking a hole in your balloon but making you anxious as well.

Pro tip:Never compare chapter 3 of yours to someone’s chapter 13. Create a balance in your life and go with the flow.


All or nothing: When was the last time you planned on doing a 30 minutes workout, did 10 minutes, resented yourself for not completing it, and never did it again? I see you feeling guilty. How many times you said to yourself, it’s either a 30 minutes workout or no workout, perfect mac and cheese or no mac and cheese, a perfect picture on Instagram, or no picture on Instagram.

Pro tip:Do your workout, even if its 10 minutes, start that blog even if you think no one reads, read that book even if its half-page a day. Don’t try to control the consequences, and see yourself feeling more content and happy.


Above mentioned examples are the needles that poke holes in your happiness balloon and surprisingly, none of them are caused by other people but ourselves. I hope that now you have a clear understanding of the quote ‘Happiness comes from within’. Now, how many of you promise to get rid of these needles from your life so that they don’t poke holes in your happiness balloon?
