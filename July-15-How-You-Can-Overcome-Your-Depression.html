How You Can Overcome Your Depression

The recent suicide of Sushant Singh Rajpoot took social media by storm when the initial news broke out of him committing suicide as a result of depression.


Within a few hours, social media flooded with posts related to depression and for the first time in a long time, the world opened their eyes to the possibility of suicide as a result of depression. On 14 th June-2020, social media saw the biggest flood of self-proclaimed therapists and suicide experts explaining how depression is a concerning matter only for the trend to die as soon as the clock strikes midnight of 15 th June.


The question here is if depression is indeed a really serious mental health issue, how one can overcome that?


The answer to this widely asked question is “talk to a psychologist/therapist’. He/she will rule out whether you are going through depression or some other mental health issue. Because of a lack of awareness about mental health issues, a lot of people go to psychologists claiming they have depression but after a few sessions, it turns out they are going through some other mental health-related issues.


If however, it's confirmed that one is going through depression and not any other mental health issue, then there are a few tips to manage depression aside from being in therapy. Therapy for depression given by psychologists and is the best way to manage and eliminate depression.


Some tips to manage depression are as follows.


Set/follow proper routine/deadline: One of the biggest symptoms of depression is lack of interest in any activities or activities that once were fun. A person in depression doesn’t feel like doing anything. In this scenario, a person must set and follow a proper routine. Once a task from the routine is done, it helps with following the routine.

Exercise: When we hear the word exercise, the thing that comes into our mind is heavy workouts, lots of cardio, and a well-equipped gym. Well, exercise is any physical activity one does even if it’s going for a walk daily. Researches have proved that people who exercise on a regular bases find significant improvement in their moods and helps them with depression.

Mindfulness: Mindfulness is “staying in the moment”. Depression stems from self-loathing on things in the past. In our minds, we create this perfect image of ourselves and when we don’t do as we are expecting from ourselves, we fall into depression. The best way to overcome is by staying in the present moment or concentrating on what you are doing instead of thinking about the past.

Drink water: When a psychologist asks a person how many glasses of water you drink, the answer is “I don’t drink much water”. Water is essential for our body and not drinking enough leads to disturbing moods. A simple act as drinking 8 glasses of water is proved to elevate the moods of people in depression.

Lastly, pat yourself on the back at the end of the day for going through depression yet coping with it every day.
